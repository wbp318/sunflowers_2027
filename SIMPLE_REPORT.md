# Sunflowers Around Lake Providence — The Short Version

**For:** Anybody thinking about growing or handling sunflowers in the Delta
**Date:** July 2026

---

## The bottom line

Sunflowers **will grow here just fine**. The problem is **nobody around here buys them**. The closest place that takes sunflower seed is about **600 miles away in Lubbock, Texas** (a birdseed company called Red River Commodities). The big oil plants are way up in North Dakota.

So the question isn't "can we grow them" — it's "who's gonna buy them and what does the truck ride cost."

## The money, simple

- Buyers were paying about **$22–23 per hundred pounds** for oil sunflowers (late 2025 prices — check current prices before planting).
- Trucking a load to Lubbock costs roughly **$1,800 a truck**, which eats about **$3.50–4.00** of that $22. You keep the rest, minus drying and handling.
- **Thin money, but real money.** And prices jump around a lot — they nearly doubled in the 2021 drought.

## Three ways to get in (easiest first)

**1. Grow it and ship it.**
Get a contract with a buyer FIRST, grow the crop, dry it, put it on a truck. Cheapest way to start — you mostly just need to dry the seed right. This proves the crop works here.

**2. Team up with a local elevator.**
A regular grain elevator can handle sunflowers — no fancy equipment needed. Two rules: dry the seed down to about **10% moisture**, and **clean the dryer every day** (sunflower fuzz catches fire in dryers — dirty equipment is what burns, not hot air). Don't do this until a buyer signs a contract.

**3. Bag your own birdseed and deer-plot seed.**
Clean it, screen it, bag it, sell it to feed stores and hunters right here. No 600-mile truck ride, and this is deer country. Costs real money to set up — maybe **$150,000–$500,000**, but honestly **nobody knows yet** because the prices we found online turned out to be junk. Get real quotes from equipment companies before believing any number.

**Best order: 1, then 2, then 3.** Prove it, pile it up, then process it.

## Free money worth chasing

- **USDA has a grant (VAPG)** that pays up to about $75,000 just to have a real feasibility study done — that's the smart first move.
- **Louisiana ag department (LDAF)** has handed out food-processing grants before ($4.6 million in 2024). Watch for the next round.

## Do this next

1. **Call Red River Commodities in Lubbock.** Ask: will you buy Louisiana sunflowers, at what price, delivered?
2. **Get a real trucking quote** for Lake Providence to Lubbock.
3. **Get written equipment quotes** — don't trust internet prices.
4. **Check current sunflower prices** at sunflowernsa.com before deciding anything.

## One warning

Every dollar figure in here is from late 2025 and prices move. **Don't plant a seed until you have a signed contract from a buyer.**
