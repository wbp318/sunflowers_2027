🌻 Sunflowers Around Lake Providence — The Short Version

For anybody thinking about growing or handling sunflowers in the Delta  •  July 2026

The bottom line: Sunflowers will grow here just fine. The problem is nobody around here buys them. The closest buyer is about 600 miles away in Lubbock, Texas (a birdseed company called Red River Commodities). The big oil plants are way up in North Dakota. So the question isn't "can we grow them" — it's "who's gonna buy them and what does the truck ride cost."

The money, simple

  • Buyers were paying about $22–23 per hundred pounds for oil sunflowers (late-2025 prices — check current prices before planting).
  • Trucking a load to Lubbock costs roughly $1,800 a truck, which eats about $3.50–$4.00 of that $22. You keep the rest, minus drying and handling.
  • Thin money, but real money. Prices jump around a lot — they nearly doubled in the 2021 drought.

Three ways to get in (easiest first)

  1. Grow it and ship it. Get a contract with a buyer FIRST, grow the crop, dry it, put it on a truck. Cheapest way to start — you mostly just need to dry the seed right. This proves the crop works here.
  2. Team up with a local elevator. A regular grain elevator can handle sunflowers — no fancy equipment needed. Two rules: dry the seed down to about 10% moisture, and clean the dryer every day (sunflower fuzz catches fire in dryers — dirty equipment is what burns, not hot air). Don't do this until a buyer signs a contract.
  3. Bag your own birdseed and deer-plot seed. Clean it, screen it, bag it, sell it to feed stores and hunters right here. No 600-mile truck ride, and this is deer country. Setup might run $150,000–$500,000, but honestly nobody knows yet — internet prices turned out to be junk. Get real quotes first.

Best order: 1, then 2, then 3. Prove it, pile it up, then process it.

Free money worth chasing

  • USDA grant (VAPG) pays up to about $75,000 just to have a real feasibility study done — the smart first move.
  • Louisiana ag department (LDAF) handed out $4.6 million in food-processing grants in 2024. Watch for the next round.

Do this next

  1. Call Red River Commodities (Lubbock): will you buy Louisiana sunflowers, at what price, delivered?
  2. Get a real trucking quote, Lake Providence to Lubbock.
  3. Get written equipment quotes — don't trust internet prices.
  4. Check current prices at sunflowernsa.com before deciding anything.
⚠  Every dollar figure here is from late 2025 and prices move. Don't plant a seed until you have a signed contract from a buyer.
